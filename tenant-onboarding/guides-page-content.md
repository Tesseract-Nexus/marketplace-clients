## Store Setup Checklist

### Heading: The launch-ready checklist

**Subheading:** Print this out or save it somewhere visible. Checking these boxes means you're ready to start selling.

**Before You Launch:**

**Store Foundation**
- [ ] Store name and logo uploaded
- [ ] Brand colors and fonts customized
- [ ] About Us page written (people read this more than you think)
- [ ] Contact information added
- [ ] Shipping policy written
- [ ] Refund/return policy created
- [ ] Privacy policy added (required by law)
- [ ] Terms of service published

**Products**
- [ ] At least 10 products added (fewer makes your store look empty)
- [ ] High-quality product photos uploaded (minimum 3 per product)
- [ ] Product descriptions written (focus on benefits, not just features)
- [ ] Prices set (including any variants like size or color)
- [ ] Inventory quantities entered
- [ ] Product categories created for easy browsing

**Payments & Shipping**
- [ ] Payment gateway connected and tested
- [ ] Shipping zones configured for your delivery areas
- [ ] Shipping rates set (or free shipping thresholds)
- [ ] Courier partners selected and integrated
- [ ] Tax settings configured (GST for Indian businesses)

**Testing**
- [ ] Placed a test order from start to finish
- [ ] Received order confirmation email
- [ ] Tested checkout on mobile (most customers shop on phones)
- [ ] Checked that payment processing works
- [ ] Verified shipping calculations are correct

**Marketing Setup**
- [ ] Google Analytics connected (track your traffic)
- [ ] Newsletter signup form added to homepage
- [ ] Social media links added to footer
- [ ] First promotional campaign planned
- [ ] Email templates customized (order confirmations, shipping updates)

## Your First Sale Guide

### Heading: From zero to first customer

**Estimated time:** 30 minutes to read, ongoing to implement

**What this guide covers:**
You've launched your store. Now what? This guide shows you how to get your first paying customer without spending a fortune on ads.

**Key sections:**

**Week 1: Tell everyone you know**
- Sharing on personal social media (without being annoying)
- Reaching out to friends and family (yes, really)
- Local community groups and forums
- WhatsApp status and Instagram stories

**Week 2: Organic social media**
- Posting product photos that get attention
- Using hashtags that actually work
- Engaging with potential customers (not just broadcasting)
- Collaborating with micro-influencers in your niche

**Week 3: Content that attracts buyers**
- Writing helpful blog posts related to your products
- Creating simple how-to videos
- Answering questions on forums and communities
- Building trust before asking for the sale

**Week 4: First paid promotion**
- Starting small with Instagram or Facebook ads (₹500 test budget)
- Targeting the right audience
- Creating offers that convert new visitors
- Measuring what works

**The psychology of your first customer:**
New stores face a trust problem. No reviews, no social proof, no track record. Here's how to overcome it:
- Offer a first-purchase discount (give people a reason to take a chance)
- Provide exceptional service to those early customers
- Ask for reviews and testimonials
- Share behind-the-scenes content (show the human behind the store)

## Marketing Basics Guide

### Heading: Marketing that doesn't feel sleazy

**Estimated time:** 40 minutes

**What you'll learn:**
Marketing doesn't have to mean pushy sales tactics. This guide shows you how to attract customers by being helpful, honest, and human.

**Core topics covered:**

**1. Know who you're selling to**
You can't market to everyone. Define your ideal customer and speak directly to them.
- Creating simple customer personas
- Understanding pain points and desires
- Finding where your customers already hang out online

**2. Build trust before asking for money**
People buy from businesses they trust. Here's how to earn it.
- Sharing your story (why you started this)
- Providing value before making offers
- Being transparent about pricing and policies
- Responding quickly to questions and concerns

**3. Email marketing that people actually open**
Email isn't dead – boring emails are.
- Building your list without buying contacts
- Writing subject lines people can't ignore
- Sending emails people want to read (not just promotions)
- Abandoned cart recovery that converts

**4. Social media without burning out**
You don't need to be on every platform. Pick one or two and do them well.
- Choosing the right platforms for your products
- Creating a simple content calendar
- Engaging authentically with followers
- Using user-generated content
- When to go live vs. posting scheduled content

**5. SEO in plain English**
Getting found on Google without the technical headaches.
- Writing product descriptions Google loves
- Using the right keywords naturally
- Getting other sites to link to yours
- Optimizing for local search

**6. Paid advertising on a budget**
When you're ready to spend money, spend it smart.
- Starting with ₹1000 and testing what works
- Facebook/Instagram ads for beginners
- Google Shopping setup
- Retargeting people who visited but didn't buy

**7. Turning customers into fans**
Your best marketing is happy customers telling their friends.
- Creating referral programs that work
- Asking for reviews (and responding to them)
- Building a community around your brand
- Surprise-and-delight moments
